# ✔️ How React Flow can help you?

In this article, we will show you how React Flow can help advance your store work and grow your business faster.

Automation of many routine tasks is an important key to success. Thanks to it, you have more spare time for things that really determine the development of your business while all the necessary processes are done independently.

****Let's look at this situation:****

A store owner wants to reward the customers who have over 50 orders in his store, and send an automatic email to them with a discount coupon. But he is tired to track such customers manually and spending hours sending emails to each of them.

So, the solution to this problem is the next workflow:

1.  Click on ****"Create new"**** in a Dashboard to create start creating a new workflow. After that, we should define when this workflow will launch - after the ****trigger action****. Let's select the ****"Order paid"**** trigger that will launch our workflow when the customer has already paid it.
    
2.  To find out if this customer has this count of orders we need to add a condition to verify it. Click on plus → click ****"Condition"**** button and select the ****"Orders count"**** condition in the ****Order/Customer**** block.
    

![](https://cdn.heymantle.com/docs/screenshots/4f24ffa7-ad9d-4c04-9c45-c2556234eaef/add-condition-react-flow_1uvpam2.png)

![](https://cdn.heymantle.com/docs/screenshots/68a84c68-50e1-4587-8cbf-a987648af88d/orders-count-condition_1lhemrw.png)

3.  The next step is to set up the necessary value in the field on the right. First, select the ****"greater or equal to"**** matcher and put ****"50"**** below. We've just set the condition that will check the number of customer orders.

![](https://cdn.heymantle.com/docs/screenshots/713bb5c6-d4d8-47cf-8c90-47aa4aa42640/set-number-of-orders_3dxr68.png)

4.  Now we need to add an action to send an email with a discount coupon. But first of all, let's set a delay after which this email will automatically sent to the customer. Click on the plus near “otherwise” → click "Delay" button and configure its time interval (hours or days) and its value. Let's set 2 hours from the workflow start.

![](https://cdn.heymantle.com/docs/screenshots/3bed1886-9ccc-4eef-856d-5c5afa1ad798/select-delay_4zrr0b.png)

5.  Finally, we can add an action to end the workflow. Click on plus near “then” → click on “Action”. Go down to the ****External**** actions and select the ****"Send email"****. It's easy to compose an email right here in the builder.

![](https://cdn.heymantle.com/docs/screenshots/34f1501c-92f0-4867-b32a-d3abd2329bf1/select-send-email-action_1o18x02.png)

Provide the necessary email addresses and write the subject and the message itself in the fields on the right.

![](https://cdn.heymantle.com/docs/screenshots/0066f8de-6e08-4ebd-bd16-eca7b254b80f/set-email-info_1aaw916.png)

6.  Now, we should save all the changes. Click on the ****"Save"**** button and name this workflow.
    
7.  Congrats! Everything is set and our workflow is ready to run. Click the ****"Enable"**** button on the top and launch it!
    

# ✔️ Final result

So, with this workflow the store owner has identified the most loyal customers and, now, will reward them with the most relevant and beneficial gifts in the future, e.g., disсounts or bonuses. As a result, this workflow has improved his conversion rates, grown his sales, and built even more customer loyalty.
